Even though the name of 'Agile Project' sounds sexy, and there is a huge trend by big organisations to adopt daily scrum ceremonies, retro meetings, Refinement meetings, etc and it is marketed by concultancies. We believe you can't paint all with the same brush.
Agile projects are good if it is executed correct mainly for the large institutions as they have quite a number of teams. A standardised approach will let mid-level managers to follow projects.
Another very useful area of Agile Projects, where the product owner has a very little idea of what he/she wants to achive. The continious feedbacks, and as a result of it changes , shift in the decisions and functions take place very often.
Finally, we find it very useful through the end of a project, when it's time to put everything together for any projects too.
However on the otherhand, Marketted Agile Project implementations will be a massive time and money loss for the Startups which already have a very agile team naturally due to its size. Giving the same project development advise to Big and Small companies are no different than advising a 'cheetah' to move like an 'elephant'. It may work for elephant and keep their tribe speed faster, but that will be a disaster for a cheetah family.
